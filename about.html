Equipment

Click on the image and go left or right to see individual components of my setup. As of April 2026, I use a Canon EOS Rebel T7 body with a Laowa 100mm f/2.8 2x macro lens, a KaKas K-808 twin flash, and a homemade tracing paper diffuser. This setup was based heavily off of Thomas Shahan's review of this lens. All of these materials put together, at stock price, will run about $1000. However, it's very possible to get good deals, or even better parts, for cheaper off of secondhand marketplaces. It's what I did.

I used to be more sane

As the title implies, I wasn't always obsessed with insects to the point of picking out bad produce in the hopes that it would yield subjects. Believe it or not, in high school I didn't have the slightest interest in nature. It's hard even finding any pics I took that coincidentally focused on natural elements. I must have been more interested in other fields, like fashion.

Perhaps even more surprisingly, in middle school I was practically allergic to touching grass. All of my free time was spent gaming. Regrettably, I have spent days out of my life exclusively playing Fortnite, and most of my hours are from back then. I wasn't even good enough to make earnings.

For as little as I pursued nature in high school, at least I left my room fairly often. My friends and I ended up getting into urban exploration, which is where my disregard for property lines comes from. That's given me some rare finds and excellent memories, though admittedly we did get into some sticky situations.

It was on these adventures that I bonded with my very first mentor, my classmate and now friend Yann. He shocked us with the news that he was in the top 3 fish identifiers globally on iNaturalist. He would point out all the different moths we came across in our nighttime urbexcursions, and over time, he wore me down to the point that I was now familiar with the app iNaturalist.

I didn't take after Yann immediately. It took a semester of mind-numbing Gen-Ed prerequisite classes in my freshman year of college for my boredom to take the reins. For once, something good came out of that. I started walking to parks like the one pictured here, and it was genuinely amazing to immerse myself in an aspect of the world that I had neglected for so long.

And that pretty much brings us to the present day! My love for most forms of life has completely taken over my free time. I run the University of Michigan's Entomology Club, and I am mentoring several up-and-coming naturalists, including my own brother! Tap on the image of him and go to the next one for an inspiring before-and-after.

These days, you can find me prowling around the bottom floor of my apartment building in search of jumping spiders, moths, etc., as well as driving all across the Midwest in pursuit of rare wildflowers to photograph!
